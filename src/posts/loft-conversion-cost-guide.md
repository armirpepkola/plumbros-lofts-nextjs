"How much does a loft conversion cost?" It's the first question every London homeowner asks, and the most difficult to answer with a single number. While some companies might give you a vague estimate, the truth is that the final cost depends on a huge range of factors.

This guide provides a transparent, no-nonsense breakdown of the real costs involved in a London loft conversion in 2025, so you can budget with confidence and understand exactly what you're paying for.

### The Main Factors Influencing Your Final Quote

The price of your loft conversion will primarily be determined by four key things:

1.  **The Type of Conversion:** A simple Velux conversion is the cheapest, while a complex mansard is the most expensive. Dormers sit in the middle and offer the best value for money in terms of space gained.
2.  **The Size of Your Property:** A larger loft simply requires more materials, more structural work, and more labour, all of which increase the cost.
3.  **The Specification and Finish:** The choices you make for your bathroom (e.g., standard fixtures vs. designer marble), windows, flooring, and lighting will have a significant impact on the final price.
4.  **Structural Complexity:** Some properties may require additional steel beams or more complex structural work to support the new floor, which will be identified by a structural engineer.

To give you a realistic idea, here are the typical price ranges you can expect for different types of loft conversions in London. These figures include design, labour, materials, and a standard 'plastered' finish, but exclude VAT.

| Conversion Type                | Estimated Price Range (Excl. VAT) |
| ------------------------------ | --------------------------------- |
| Velux (Rooflight) Conversion   | £25,000 - £40,000                 |
| Dormer Conversion (Rear/Side)  | £45,000 - £70,000                 |

| Mansard / L-Shaped Dormer      | £60,000 - £90,000+                |

### The "Hidden" Costs Homeowners Often Forget

Be sure to clarify if these are included in any quote you receive:

*   **VAT:** Always confirm if your quote includes or excludes VAT at the current rate of 20%.
*   **Party Wall Agreements:** If you need to hire a surveyor for your neighbour(s), this can cost £700 - £1,500 per neighbour.
*   **Decorating:** Most quotes cover a ready-to-paint plastered finish. Painting, wallpapering, and final flooring installation are usually extra.
*   **Interior Fittings:** Things like built-in wardrobes or shelving are typically custom additions outside of the main build cost.
